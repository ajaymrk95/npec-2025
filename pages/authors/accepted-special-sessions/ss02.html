Technical Outline of the Session:

Transforming Power: Intelligent Control of WBG Converters" highlights a crucial advancement in modern power electronics. Wide Bandgap (WBG) semiconductors, such as SiC and GaN, offer superior efficiency, higher switching frequencies, and greater thermal stability compared to traditional silicon-based devices. Intelligent control strategies enhance the performance, reliability, and adaptability of these converters in complex, dynamic environments. This synergy enables significant improvements in applications ranging from renewable energy systems to electric vehicles and smart grids. By unlocking the full potential of WBG technologies, intelligent control is pivotal in driving the next generation of high-performance, energy-efficient power conversion systems.

Topics of Session:

The original contributions, from different perspectives, including Ph.D Students , PG & UG Students, Academicians, researchers,and professional communities are invited under (but not limtied to) under the following topics :

  • Design and control of SiC/GaN-based converters
  • Integration of AI in WBG converter control
  • WBG converters for smart grid and microgrid applications
  • Grid-interactive renewable energy systems
  • Security implications in digitally controlled WBG systems
