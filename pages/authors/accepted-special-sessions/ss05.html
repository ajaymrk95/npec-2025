Technical Outline of the Session:

The rapid integration of renewable energy sources such as solar and wind has introduced challenges in ensuring grid stability, reliability, and efficient power delivery. Power electronics and advanced control techniques are central to addressing these issues, enabling effective energy conversion, grid compliance, and improved power quality. This session focuses on recent advancements in converter topologies, modulation strategies, and intelligent control methods for renewable energy applications. Key themes include grid-forming and grid-following inverter control, predictive and adaptive strategies for handling intermittency, harmonic mitigation, and fault ride-through capabilities. The session will also highlight control frameworks for hybrid energy systems, microgrids, and the application of digital controllers and real-time simulations in renewable energy integration. By exploring these innovations, the session emphasizes how advanced power electronics and control approaches can enhance efficiency, resilience, and scalability of renewable systems, thereby accelerating the transition towards a sustainable and carbon-neutral energy future.

Topics of Session:

  • Advanced Converter Topologies for Renewable Energy Systems
  • Control Strategies for Grid-Tied Inverters and Multilevel Converters
  • Model Predictive and Adaptive Control for Renewable Integration
  • Converter-Based Support for Microgrids and Hybrid Energy Systems
